Services and Developments in Space Engineering

Definition, characterization, architecture, and requirements of Space Systems for various missions: Once a space mission is defined, it is necessary to know which subsystems are needed to carry it out, how they will be arranged within the satellite, the duration of the mission, the electrical energy required, the space environment to which it will be exposed, the accuracy needed in various sensor measurements, etc. The initial phase of developing a space mission, where the entire system is defined in a preliminary iteration based on requirements, is crucial for the future development of the mission.

Manufacturing of subsystems for "New Space": The "New Space" concept refers to a new way of undertaking space exploration and commercialization, driven by the private sector and characterized by agility, innovation, and a new vision of space as an extension of our economy and society where everything is possible. Generally, nano-satellites weighing up to several tens of kilograms are used for these activities. The manufacturing of subsystems for nano-satellites, such as navigation and orbit determination systems, angular orientation control systems (magnetometers, magnetorquers, reaction wheels, etc.), propulsion systems, and others, is in high demand worldwide as nano-satellites are increasingly used to form space missions. For reference, the number of nano-satellites launched in the past 10 years has increased from around 200 to approximately 5,000.

Programming and analysis of space missions: The satellite communicates with the ground station each time it passes over it. During this pass, the satellite receives commands to carry out the mission, downloads telemetry reporting on the satellite's status and the results of the mission, such as satellite images of Earth. Programming the mission depends on many factors, such as the satellite's orbit, the timing of the mission, the maneuvers to be performed, the preparation of the payload (the subsystem carrying out the mission), etc. Programming the mission to send commands and analyzing it requires complex algorithms located at the ground station. If it involves programming the mission, these algorithms dictate to the satellite what it must do to complete the mission. If it involves analyzing the mission, these algorithms explain to the user what happened, for example, satellite image analysis.

Algorithms and simulations for space missions: Designing a space mission requires algorithms and simulations for the feasibility study phase, the development phase, and even after launch. For example, a satellite simulator is essential for understanding the origins of failures that may occur in various satellite subsystems during the mission. There are also algorithms and logic that need to be developed for the onboard computer, such as mission initiation and completion, angular orientation control, etc. All these algorithms are highly complex and require expertise based on experience and the ability to interpret the mission's needs.

Thermal control and space environment testing: The space environment is characterized by three main factors that do not exist on Earth, which is protected by atmospheric layers: significant temperature fluctuations between satellite "day" and "night," vacuum or low atmospheric densities, and radiation (space debris or space waste, which is unpredictable, also exists in the space environment). Before launching a satellite, it is necessary to know or predict how these factors will affect it, which is achieved through simulations and tests performed on Earth. Depending on these tests and simulations, it can be determined how to best protect the satellite in space.

Solar Drones

Fixed-wing drones are increasingly used in various daily activities: pest monitoring in large-scale agriculture, topography and mapping, urbanization monitoring and control, national park monitoring and control, etc. Currently, the engines powering these drones rely on fuel, which is costly, causes atmospheric pollution, and limits flight time. Solar energy can be utilized by using solar cells that convert sunlight into electrical energy to power the drone engines and overcome the three inherent issues of current drones.
